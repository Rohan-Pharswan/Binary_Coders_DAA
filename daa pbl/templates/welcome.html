What This System Does

This Disaster Relief Supply Chain Optimization System is a specialized tool that creates and optimizes supply chain networks for disaster response scenarios. It does NOT integrate with external systems like Google Maps, alarm systems, or real-time data feeds. Instead, it operates as a standalone optimization engine that requires manual input of disaster scenario data.

The system takes predefined inputs including warehouse locations, affected areas, hospitals, shelters, available supplies, vehicle fleet, and road networks to create an optimized delivery strategy. Using advanced algorithms like the Knapsack Algorithm for supply optimization and A* pathfinding for route planning, it determines the most efficient way to deliver critical supplies to disaster-affected areas.

Key Capabilities: The system optimizes supply allocation, vehicle routing, and delivery scheduling based on priority levels, vehicle capacities, and road conditions. It provides visual representations of the optimized network and allows real-time modifications to adapt to changing disaster conditions.

System Assumptions & Pre-conditions

This system operates under specific assumptions that define its scope and capabilities. Understanding these assumptions is crucial for proper system utilization and realistic expectations of its performance.

1
Graph-Based Disaster Representation
Disaster areas are represented as mathematical graphs where locations (warehouses, affected areas, hospitals) are nodes, and roads are edges with weights representing time, cost, or risk factors.
2
Known Road Conditions
Road conditions (open, blocked, or risky) are known or can be updated in real-time. Updates can be provided manually or through simulated data rather than external monitoring systems.
3
Available Demand Data
Each affected area has predefined needs for essential supplies (food, water, medicine, blankets, etc.) which are provided as input data rather than real-time assessment.
4
Limited Vehicle Fleet & Supply Constraints
Vehicles have fixed capacity limits, and supplies must be optimized for delivery using the Knapsack Algorithm to maximize value within capacity constraints.
5
Priority-Based Delivery System
Hospitals and emergency shelters are served before residential or low-risk areas, implementing a hierarchical delivery priority system.
6
Manual Input Requirements
The system requires manual input of all scenario data including locations, supplies, vehicles, and road networks. It does not automatically gather data from external sources.
7
Reliable Travel Time Estimates
Road weights (travel times) are assumed to be reliable and are based on distance and known conditions rather than real-time traffic data.
8
Single Disaster Scenario Focus
The system is designed to respond to one disaster type (earthquake, flood, etc.) at a time rather than managing multiple simultaneous crises.
